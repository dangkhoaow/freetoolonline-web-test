Pick PNG when each separated still will be edited, layered, or archived without further compression - the alpha channel and every pixel survive the export intact. Pick JPG when the destination is a slide deck or a quick social repost and a leaner payload matters more than per-pixel fidelity. The FPS setting then decides how many frames per second of the source GIF land on disk, so a longer hold time at 10 fps produces a sparser set than the same loop sampled at 24.
