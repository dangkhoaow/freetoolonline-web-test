A "how to compress a zip file" question almost always comes from one of two corners: the recipient needs a single attachment instead of a folder of loose files, or the recipient has an attachment size cap and the original folder is too big. The three-step recipe on this page handles the first corner directly; the second corner needs a re-encoding step on the media files before they go into the archive, because already-compressed formats like JPG, MP4, and MP3 do not shrink much when packaged into a .zip.

For the size-cap variant of the question, the companion guide how to compress a zip file to a specific size (2 MB / 25 MB / 100 KB) walks through the quality-versus-bytes choices that land the final .zip under the cap your inbox or upload form imposes.
