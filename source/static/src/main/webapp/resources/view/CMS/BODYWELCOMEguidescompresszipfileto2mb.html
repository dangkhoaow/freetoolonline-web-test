Compress a ZIP file to 2 MB: free guide on which inputs hit the 2 MB enterprise-SMTP attachment cap on their own and when the package must be split.

The 2 MB ceiling is almost always the receiving side's enterprise-SMTP attachment cap, not a goal the sender chose freely - corporate Exchange installs, SMTP relays running spam-filter rules, legacy webmail interfaces, and contact-form upload widgets pin the inbound limit at 2 MB. Knowing the cap belongs to the destination, not to the format, is what makes the split-package strategy work: one compressible-text ZIP under 2 MB plus the media files routed through a share link the recipient's gateway does accept.

How to compress a ZIP file to 2 MB has three sub-questions, and they sit in this order. First, which inputs pack down: text-like content (logs, CSV, JSON, source code, configuration) compresses aggressively because DEFLATE strips long runs of repeated bytes, while JPG photos, MP4 clips, MP3 audio, and most PDFs barely shrink because their own codec already removed the redundancy. Second, what the compression level slider does and does not do: Maximum and Ultra add CPU time but rarely shave more than a few extra percent past the default pass, so the slider mostly changes build time, not whether the archive crosses the 2 MB line. Third, when to split the email-attachment delivery: when a mixed-media folder still will not pack under the cap, send the text-like inputs as one small ZIP that does fit, and route the media files through a share link the recipient's gateway accepts.

How to compress a ZIP file to 2 MB - the short version of the three sub-questions above - is: read which inputs pack down to the 2 MB attachment cap, understand what the compression level slider actually does at the margin, and recognise when to split the email-attachment delivery into a text ZIP plus a share link for the media.

A quick decision checklist for the 2 MB target: separate the text-like files (CSV, JSON, logs, source code) from the media files (photos, video, audio, PDFs) before compressing, build one ZIP from the text-like set with the default compression level, and only escalate the slider to Maximum if the result is within a few percent of 2 MB. If the text-only archive already exceeds 2 MB, the slider will not close that gap - split the bundle into two ZIPs and send the larger half through the share-link delivery path the receiving SMTP gateway accepts. Media-heavy folders should not start at the slider at all; route them through the share link from the outset and keep the 2 MB email attachment reserved for the text-like manifest.
