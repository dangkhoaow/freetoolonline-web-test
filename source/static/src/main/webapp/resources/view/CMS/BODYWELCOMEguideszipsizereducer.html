A reader who types "zip size reducer" usually means one of three things: bundle a multi-file project into one archive smaller than the inputs combined, attach a folder that an email server otherwise refuses for being too large, or lock the archive with a password before sending it. The in-browser tool covers all three on the same upload-zip-download round trip; this guide explains which of the three the reducer can actually deliver size on, and which one is the format-specific compressor's job rather than the ZIP creator's.
