Convert iPhone Photos to JPG Supporting iOS 18

Last updated: Nov 20, 2024

HEIC, the high-efficiency image format introduced with iOS 11, continues to evolve with iOS 18, now supporting advanced features like gain map images. These advancements ensure higher image quality while optimizing file size. However, compatibility issues persist with non-Apple platforms, making a reliable converter essential.

How to change HEIC to JPG (3 steps)

Change HEIC to JPG turns the .heic photo your iPhone took into a .jpg copy that opens on any device, browser, or upload form.

  1. Open the converter on this page.
  2. Drop the .heic photo onto the upload zone (or pick it from Files).
  3. Download the JPG copy. The original .heic stays untouched.

Files upload over HTTPS for fast server-side decoding, then come back as JPG. No account or sign-up. Tick "Keep EXIF" to preserve the camera metadata and auto-rotate to match the original orientation.

When NOT to convert HEIC to JPG. Keep the HEIC original if any of these apply:

  • iCloud Photo Library sync. iCloud stores HEIC at roughly half the JPG size; converting doubles your storage bill.
  • Live Photo, Portrait Mode, or HDR gain map. The depth map, motion clip, and tone-mapping metadata are dropped when you export to JPG.
  • Round-tripping in Apple Photos or Affinity. Keep HEIC while you are still editing; convert only before export to a recipient.

When to convert HEIC to JPG

Convert when sharing with non-Apple devices, uploading to websites that only accept JPG, emailing attachments, or editing in older software. JPG is the universal format that opens everywhere without codec installs.


Privacy

Your HEIC photos upload over HTTPS to the freetoolonline service for server-side decoding. The output JPG (or PNG / WebP / combined PDF) is delivered back to your browser as a download. Files are removed from the server after a short retention window; no account is required and the original file on your device is never modified. Large batches take longer because each file is decoded server-side in turn.
