The phrase "zip password recovery online" carries a hope the math does not honour. Strong ZIP encryption (AES-128 or AES-256) has no shortcut, and a browser is the wrong environment for the brute-force attack that does exist for legacy ZipCrypto archives. The recovery paths that actually work are not crack paths - they are "unlock with the password you have," "re-create the archive from your source files," or "ask the sender." Reading the three before clicking through saves an upload to a site that cannot deliver what its homepage promises.

The companion tool on this site is the Remove ZIP Password unlocker. It removes a known password from an archive (producing a copy that opens without the password); it does not guess unknown passwords. If the archive was built from files you still have locally, the Compress ZIP File creator builds a fresh archive with a password you control - skipping the locked original entirely. Either route is concrete; "online crack" is not.
