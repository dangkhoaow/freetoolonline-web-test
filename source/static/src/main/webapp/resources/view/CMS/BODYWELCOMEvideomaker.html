Image sequence to MP4: what the tool does behind the scenes

Video Maker takes the still images you upload, places them on a timeline in upload order, and renders each frame for the duration you set (1–5 seconds each). Between frames the tool applies a transition (fade or zoom); at the end it encodes the whole sequence into an MP4 container using an H.264 stream so the output plays in every modern browser, phone, and video player without conversion. If you enable audio, an uploaded track is mixed in at export time and trimmed to the slideshow duration. The resolution you pick (320×240 through 1680×1050) becomes the MP4’s native size; higher resolutions cost more encode time but look sharper on retina screens.


Picking a frame duration and resolution that match the intent

Social recap (2–3s per frame, 1280×720). Use the default 3-second frame with a 16:9 resolution so each image has time to read without the video feeling slow. Keep total duration under 60 seconds for Instagram, TikTok, and LinkedIn autoplay.

Product announcement (4–5s per frame, 1600×900). Longer frames let viewers absorb specs or a short caption. Pick a 16:9 resolution for site embeds and email previews.

Personal memory / family recap (3s per frame, 1280×720 or 1440×900). A comfortable middle ground. 16:10 or 16:9 works well for laptop playback.

Event signage (1–2s per frame, 800×600). Faster cadence feels alive on a kiosk display. Lower resolution keeps file size small if the screen is SD.

Audio matters. If you include music, pick a track whose length matches your total slideshow duration (frame count × frame duration). Mismatched audio is either cut mid-note or plays over a black tail.


Privacy: your images and audio stay on your device

Video Maker runs in your browser via the Canvas API and WebCodecs. The images you upload are read locally, drawn onto a canvas, and encoded into the MP4 on the page. Nothing is sent to a server; when you close the tab, the uploaded images and the generated MP4 are both released from memory. That makes the tool safe for family photos, internal product mockups, or pre-announcement campaign material you can’t yet share publicly.
