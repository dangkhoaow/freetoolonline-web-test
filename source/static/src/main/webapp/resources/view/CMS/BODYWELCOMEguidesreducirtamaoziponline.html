One quick expectation-setter before you click through to the creator. The phrase reducir tamaño zip online sets up a size-reduction goal, but the actual size cut comes from the input side, not from any slider on the creator page. A bundle of text-heavy inputs (logs, CSV, JSON, source code) typically lands at 30 to 60 percent of the raw bytes inside a .zip; a bundle of already-compressed media (JPG, MP4, MP3, most PDFs) lands within 2 to 5 percent of the raw bytes because the codec already compressed those streams once. The level slider on the creator changes how long the archive takes to build, not whether it shrinks.
