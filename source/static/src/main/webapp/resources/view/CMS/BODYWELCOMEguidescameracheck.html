A "camera check" is the synonym most people reach for when they want to confirm that the webcam works before a meeting or after plugging in a new device. The check itself is the same in-browser preview the camera-test tool drives - a one-click flow that asks for the video stream, shows you the live frame, and releases the device as soon as you leave the page. Read the guide above for the synonym mapping, the three end-states, and the two common failure modes that send the preview dark.

Running a full hardware sweep instead of just the camera? After the camera check, run a quick microphone test and an LCD test; together the three take under a minute and cover the surfaces a typical video call depends on.

When the preview activates but shows a black frame, the lens is usually covered, another app holds the device, or the OS driver is at fault. The companion guide camera test shows black screen: four fixes walks the four common hardware-side causes - a physical lens cover, a second app holding the device, an OS driver fault, and a stale permission state - with a verification step and an OS-specific reset path for each.
