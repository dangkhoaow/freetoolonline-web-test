Match the symptom to the right test first; it saves time on a hardware-or-permission-or-quality triage. The 30-second decision tree maps four symptom buckets to the right starting tool: no preview on a phone goes to the camera test for hardware; a blank webcam tile in a call routes to the camera test in the calls browser for permission; a soft, dark, or off-colour picture goes to the quality guide; and a mixed symptom starts at the camera test by default.

A webcam test adds checks the phone-side camera test cannot see. It covers OS and browser permission grants, the OS-level privacy toggle that silently blocks the lens, the per-tab device selector inside the page, virtual cameras such as OBS or Snap that intercept the real device, and the laptop hardware shutter switch on many business models. If the call tile stays blank after the phone test passes, that stack is where the issue usually lives.

A camera quality test grades the picture along four axes: focus, exposure, colour balance, noise. Focus grades sharpness; exposure grades brightness; colour balance grades neutrals; noise grades shadow detail. The four axes together turn "looks fine" into a concrete next step.
