Working from an exact pixel rectangle rather than a preset? Pick Free in the aspect-ratio panel and the side inputs accept the X position, Y position, Width, and Height as direct numbers. The crop box snaps to that rectangle, and the Download button writes the result at the chosen pixel dimensions in the source format. The original picture stays in the workspace after the first download, so a second crop at a different rectangle takes one click rather than a re-upload.

Zooming in before the cut helps when the crop frame needs to land on a single eye, a single label on a cluttered shelf, or a single corner of a document. The zoom controls magnify the working canvas without changing the source resolution, and the live preview pane shows the crop result at the natural output size while the frame is still being dragged - so the "is this the right ratio?" judgement happens before the Download click instead of after a re-crop on a second pass.
