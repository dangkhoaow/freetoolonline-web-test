A folder-to-zip step turns a tidy folder layout on the sender side into one neat .zip that lands as a single download on the recipient side. The reach side of the format is the whole reason this site (and most senders) default to ZIP rather than 7z or RAR: every desktop OS opens it without an install, every webmail attachment scanner understands it, and most upload widgets accept a .zip where they would refuse a .7z or .rar. A smaller archive the recipient cannot open is worse than a larger one they can.

Before you click through to the creator, group the inputs by type in your local folder so the size cut is honest: text-like inputs (notes, CSV exports, source files, JSON dumps) in one bundle, media (photos already saved as JPG, video already saved as MP4, audio already saved as MP3) in another. The text-like bundle is where the archive actually shaves bytes; the media bundle goes through the same workflow but mostly for tidy single-file delivery, not for size. Either way, the size cut comes from the inputs you grouped, not from the level slider you pick at the creator at /zip-tools/zip-file.html.
