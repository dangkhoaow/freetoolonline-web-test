A reader who types "resize zip file" into a search box has usually already tried the obvious thing - dragging the .zip into a desktop archive utility and looking for a "resize" or "shrink" button that does not exist. The verb belongs to image and video tools, not to archive tools. On this site the work happens on the input side: shrink the inputs that go into the .zip with the image and PDF tools, then pack the smaller folder on /zip-tools/zip-file.html. The size drop comes from what you put in, not from a setting on the archive itself.

For an "I just need this under 25 MB for email" answer, the size-target walkthrough is the one-page recipe. For an "I want to understand why ultra-compression does not help on a folder of photos" answer, the per-input-type shrinkage table shows where the ceiling sits.
