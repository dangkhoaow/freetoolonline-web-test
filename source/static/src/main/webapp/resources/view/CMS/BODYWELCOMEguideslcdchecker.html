An LCD checker is the verification side of the screen-defect workflow. Where the "test" wording reads as "run a test to see what the screen does", the "check" wording reads as "verify the screen is fine before I commit to it" - returning a monitor, accepting a delivery, finalising a warranty claim. The browser tool is the same in both cases; the framing the reader brings to it differs, and this guide names that gap so the routing from query to action is one click.

The companion tool is at LCD Test - a free in-browser panel-check that cycles through solid red, green, blue, white, black, and a grey gradient. Press F11 for full-screen, click through, and watch for any dot or patch that does not match. The whole pass takes about a minute. Two related screen-side checks have dedicated tools - Camera Test covers the front and rear cameras, Microphone Test covers the mic and headset routing - and a touchscreen-only digitizer check has its own paint-trail procedure documented in the linked guide.
