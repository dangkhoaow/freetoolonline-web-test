Think of "compress ZIP size" as a question with two halves: what is in the folder, and how much can DEFLATE squeeze it down. The first half almost always decides the answer. A 500 MB folder of CSV exports often packs to 80 to 150 MB inside a .zip; a 500 MB folder of holiday JPGs packs to 495 to 505 MB inside the same .zip; a mixed folder lands between the two, weighted by how much of it is text versus media.

Build the archive at /zip-tools/zip-file.html when you have decided whether the size cut is worth the trip. The creator runs in your browser, never uploads the files, and writes a standard .zip every desktop OS opens without an install. When the goal is the smallest possible delivery, split the upload before you archive: bundle the compressible inputs (text files, CSVs, source code) together for the real size win, then keep the media (photos, video, audio) in a separate folder or share path. Two smaller packages usually land lighter than one mashed-together ZIP, because the media side was already codec-compressed and wraps poorly inside a second compression layer.
