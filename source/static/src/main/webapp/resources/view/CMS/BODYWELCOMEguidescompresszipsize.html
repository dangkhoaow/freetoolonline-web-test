Think of "compress ZIP size" as a question with two halves: what is in the folder, and how much can DEFLATE squeeze it down. The first half almost always decides the answer. A 500 MB folder of CSV exports often packs to 80 to 150 MB inside a .zip; a 500 MB folder of holiday JPGs packs to 495 to 505 MB inside the same .zip; a mixed folder lands between the two, weighted by how much of it is text versus media.

Build the archive at /zip-tools/zip-file.html when you have decided whether the size cut is worth the trip. The creator runs in your browser, never uploads the files, and writes a standard .zip every desktop OS opens without an install.

The split-delivery heuristic: when the goal is the smallest possible delivery, split the upload before you archive. Zip the compressible inputs together (text, CSVs, source code) for the real size win, then handle media separately - resize, re-encode, or leave as-is. The resulting two-package delivery is usually smaller than one ZIP of everything mashed together, because media was already codec-compressed and wraps poorly inside a second compression layer.
